Project Background¶

FSGS, which is not Renal Clear Cell Carcinoma¶

  • Focal Segmental Glomerulosclerosis, or FSGS, is a leading cause of ESRD in the United States
  • There are several sub-types of FSGS. More on that later.
  • It is a progressive disease with no targeted therapies
  • The disease is characterized by its pathognomonic histological pattern, which is focal and segmental scarring of the glomerulus

FSGS Sub-types that result in the histology¶

  • Primary FSGS: Idiopathic, thought to be caused by some circulating factor
  • Adaptive/Secondary FSGS: Caused by a mismatch between glomerular demand and capacity
    • Born pre-mature (less nephrons per kidney) adulthood obesity (high-metabolic load)
  • High-Penetrance Genetic FSGS
  • HIV-Associated Nephropathy (HIVAN): Collapsing Variant of FSGS
  • Drug-Induced FSGS: Nephrotoxic drug-related damage to glomerulus

Why Sub-types are important in this case¶

  • There are decisions related to the drug-regimen for each of the subtypes of FSGS
  • The actual categories are controversial and are an active field of study
  • Taxonomy of Kidney-Disease can be very difficult in general

The plan: Statistically-robust clustering of disease phenotypes¶

  • This requires a complete dataset of FSGS patient data... which I am still acquiring
  • In the meantime, the plan is to build out the pipeline on a public dataset of Renal Clear Cell Carcinoma data

Yes, there is¶

  • Three of the event-time type variables correlate with one another
    • Age at Diagnosis correlates with Age at Death because someone who is diagnosed when they are old may just live to be older
    • Age at Death correlates with Year of Birth. We suspect this is a sample bias. The patients were recruited in a recent period of time, and therefore the cross-section of patients examined would only include people who were diagnosed young and died young, and people who were diagnosed old and died when they were old.
    • Age at Diagnosis correlates with Year of Birth for a reason we have not determined

The variables that are all correlating with eachother are typical variables when trying to collect data for a Kaplan Meier Curve, which we have included later in the presentation

from lifelines import KaplanMeierFitter
# T = age at death (or 35625 (max ageatdeath + 1) if alive)
T = np.asarray(clinical_info['age_at_death'])
T[np.isnan(T)] = 35625.0

# E is array where entry = false if still alive and true if dead
E = T != 35625.0

# plot different groups

grouped_kmf = pd.DataFrame()
grouped_kmf['T'] = T
grouped_kmf['E'] = E
grouped_kmf['group'] = clinical_info['tumor_stage']

ax = plt.subplot(111)

kmf = KaplanMeierFitter()

for name, grouped_df in grouped_kmf.groupby('group'):
    kmf.fit(grouped_df["T"], grouped_df["E"], label=name)
    kmf.plot(ax=ax)

ax.set_ybound(0,1.1)
ax.set_xlabel("Age in Days")
ax.set_ylabel("Percent Survival")
ax.set_title("Survival Curve\n")
